Who Can Donate

Eligibility Criteria

VitalDrop follows WHO and Bangladesh DGDA guidelines to ensure safety for donors and recipients alike.

You Can Donate If
  • Age between  18 and 65 years
  • Weight  50 kg (110 lbs) or above
  • Hemoglobin ≥ 12.5 g/dL (women) · ≥ 13.0 g/dL (men)
  • Generally healthy with no active fever or infection
  • At least  3 months since your last whole-blood donation
  • No high-risk behavior in the past 3–12 months
You Cannot Donate If
  • Under 18 or over 65 years old
  • Weight is below 50 kg
  • Currently pregnant or breastfeeding (within 6 months)
  • Positive for HIV, Hepatitis B/C, or Syphilis
  • History of heart disease, cancer, or chronic kidney disease
  • Recent tattoo, piercing, or acupuncture (within 6 months)
  • Active cold, flu, or any infection
  • On certain medications — consult a doctor first
Bangladesh Note: All donors receive a free confidential screening — hemoglobin, blood pressure, and medical history — before donation. Final eligibility is confirmed by the medical officer on duty.

Rh Factor Explained

The Rh factor is a protein on red blood cells. Rh− blood is safe for both Rh+ and Rh− patients. Rh+ blood should only go to Rh+ patients — making O− donations especially critical in emergencies.

Critical Blood Types in BD

O− and B+ are the most urgently needed across Bangladesh's hospitals. AB− is the rarest blood type nationally.

Plasma Donation

AB+ donors are universal plasma donors — their plasma can help any patient regardless of blood type, making them doubly valuable.

Be Prepared

Before & After
Guidelines

Small preparations make a big difference to your experience and recovery.

Before Donation

  • Eat a nutritious meal 2–3 hours before
  • Drink at least 500 ml of water beforehand
  • Avoid fatty foods, alcohol, and caffeine
  • Get at least 7 hours of sleep the night before
  • Bring a valid photo ID (NID / passport)
  • Wear comfortable clothing with easy sleeve access

After Donation

  • Rest 10–15 minutes at the center — don't rush
  • Drink extra fluids for the next 24–48 hours
  • Avoid strenuous exercise or heavy lifting for 24 hours
  • Eat iron-rich foods: spinach, lentils, red meat, dates
  • Avoid alcohol for at least 24 hours post-donation
  • If you feel faint or unwell, contact us immediately
